About the Air Quality Data Viewer

The Air Quality Data Viewer is an essential tool for understanding air pollution and its impact on health. Here's what you need to know:

  • Air Pollution Risk: Air pollution is now the world’s fourth-largest risk factor for early death. In 2019, 4.5 million deaths were linked to outdoor air pollution exposures, and another 2.2 million deaths were caused by indoor air pollution.
  • Particulate Matter (PM2.5): PM2.5 is the most dangerous pollutant. It can penetrate deep into the lungs and cause various health issues, including ischemic heart disease, lung cancer, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), lower-respiratory infections (such as pneumonia), stroke, type 2 diabetes, and adverse birth outcomes.
  • Precautions: To reduce pollution exposure, consider the following:
  • Avoid strenuous physical activity outside when the air quality is bad.
  • Check our air quality map to know when air pollution outside is unsafe.
  • Wear a pollution mask if you’re going to be outside exercising in polluted air.
  • Plant trees and advocate for more greenery in public spaces.
  • Drive less and demand better public transit options, safe bike lanes, and shaded sidewalks.
